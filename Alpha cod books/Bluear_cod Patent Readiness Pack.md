## What Must Usually Be True For A U.S. Utility Patent

The invention generally needs to be:
- useful
- new
- non-obvious
- described clearly enough to make and use
- within patent-eligible subject matter

Software warning:
- not all software ideas are patent-eligible
- abstract ideas are a major risk area
- Alpha is more likely to be argued as patentable if the claim focus is on a specific technical architecture, interpreter design, multi-language execution bridge, safe runtime model, or concrete computing improvement

## Current Alpha Areas That Might Be Framed As Invention Points

Possible invention themes for a patent drafter to evaluate:

## Public Disclosure Risk

As of April 1, 2026:
- if Alpha was first publicly disclosed before April 1, 2025, U.S. patent rights may already be barred
- if Alpha was first publicly disclosed on or after April 1, 2025, there may still be a U.S. grace-period path
- foreign rights may be more limited after public disclosure
